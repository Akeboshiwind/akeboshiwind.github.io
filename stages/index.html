A scribble: Stages

2026-03-23 · updated 2026-03-23 · history

Like most others who have tried to keep a “blog”, I find myself with an itch to write something more than I do, and when I do with more drafts than posts.

With the advent of Claude Code and inspired by Simon Willison I started building more tools.

Given the above I decided to have a go at a Digital Garden.

Every Digital Garden needs some indication of the following for its items:

  • How “done” they are
  • How much effort you put in
  • How much you still identify/agree with it

I conflate all of these into three stages: scribble, draft and tale. The idea being that if I put enough effort in to take it to tale then I probably still agree with it. I can see that changing with time though.

A scribble

A note quickly jotted down on the back of a napkin. Unpolished.

If it’s a tool beware of bugs (and let me know about them!)

A draft

More thought than just the initial idea has gone into it, but I’m still not confident enough to call it done or show it around.

A tale

I’ve put a fair bit of thought and effort into this so it’s a tale I’m happy enough with to tell. But like all good tales, it may evolve over time as it’s told and re-told.
